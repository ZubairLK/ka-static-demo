Video Title : Rembrandt, Aristotle with a Bust of Homer

Video Description : Met curator Walter Liedtke on reflection in Rembrandt van Rijn’s Aristotle with a Bust of Homer, 1653 Aristotle (384–322 B.C.E.) rests his hand reflectively on a bust of Homer, the blind epic poet of the Iliad and the Odyssey. A medallion representing Alexander the Great, whom Aristotle tutored, hangs from the heavy gold chain. The philosopher contemplates material rewards as opposed to spiritual values, with the play of light and shadow on his features suggesting the motions of his mind. Painted for the great Sicilian collector Antonio Ruffo, the picture also refers to Aristotle's comparison of touch and sight as a means of acquiring knowledge.  View this work on metmuseum.org.
