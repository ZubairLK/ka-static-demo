Video Title : "Immigrant Movement International": Nationalism and immigration in the gallery

Video Description : This video brought to you by Tate.org.uk "When you are doing political work, ambiguity is very dangerous. How do you balance clarity with enough emotion with sufficient creative space?" In another performance by Cuban artist Tania Bruguera, we are confronted with questions about immigration, nationalism, and what it means to be a citizen of the world. After lining up to visit the exhibition, some visitors are required to pass an “immigration test” and a polygraph test before being granted access to the inner gallery. Once inside, the darkened space is lit only by the sparks of a migrant worker welding a sign. The sign reads “Arbeit Macht Frei” (“work makes you free”)–the very same words that hung over the entrance to the Nazi concentration camp in Auschwitz. In this case the gallery provides a more secluded space in which to confront and contemplate issues of discrimination and nationalism. What do you think Bruguera is trying to say with this piece?
